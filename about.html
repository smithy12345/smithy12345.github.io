About Me

Hello! Here is a bit about me- I was born in Illinois, lived in California until I was 4, and then moved back the Chicago-land area. Started ice skating at age 5. Figure skating and Synchronized skating have been a big part of my life and continue to do so. At the end of middle school, I made the big leap to what is called USFSA synchronized skating teams. Teams not only have the opportunity to compete around the country, but also compete internationally. By the end of my sr. year in year high school, I had competed internationally at 5 international competitions across Europe and medaled 6 times at the National Synchronized Skating Championships.
I continued skating at the University of Delaware. While skating on the synchronized skating team at Delaware, I joined the student board in charge of the team. I ran and won the webmaster position for the 2011-2012 season. Having only doodled around with myspace growing up, I had never fully tried my hand at web design. I was quickly immersed into the program used by the previous webmaster called Kompozer. While it gave the option to both code and use a non-coding option, I set sails on taking the opportunity to learn how to use the program efficiently. I spent most of the summer before my sophomore year creating a new website for the team. Learning how anchors worked, inserting images and learning the code to add videos to our website, were some of the challenges I faced. The website was such a hit with our team that the style and layout was used for the next two seasons until the university decided to have students use a university controlled platform so that all club team's sites look the same. This experience at university was a slice of peaking my interest in coding.
